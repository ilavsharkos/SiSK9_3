Round tables

What is a round table?

One of the novelties of this year’s Symposium is the round tables. Round tables are a format for discussion or lectures on a specific topic involving multiple participants. The goal is to encourage dialogue and the exchange of ideas among speakers on a defined area of science or life. The atmosphere is less formal than at traditional lectures, aiming to encourage participants to express themselves more freely, while also inviting the audience to actively engage in the conversation. We look forward to hearing your contributions at these round tables!

Science vs. industry

The first round table, "Science vs. Industry," will take place on Friday, October 11th. Our esteemed speakers, Ernest Meštrović, Ivica Komočar, Kristina Marić, Marijana Đaković, Marina Martinić Kavur, and Stribor Marković, will discuss key issues concerning the relationship between scientific research and industrial practice.


Some of the topics you can expect to hear about at this round table include employment in industry versus educational institutions, employer expectations and potential employee challenges, as well as personal experiences of our speakers regarding the clash and connection between industry and science.

Student tips & tricks

The second round table, "Student Tips & Tricks," will be held on Saturday, October 12th. Lea Raos, Antun Zelić, Lucija Marić, and Lea Komočar will share their experiences regarding studying, internships, and employment abroad. Their perspectives and advice will provide students with better insights into various opportunities and pathways that their education and future advancement can take.


Join us at this round table if you want to learn about employment opportunities abroad, the pros and cons of student exchange programs, studying outside Croatia, and how to apply the knowledge gained during studies "in both arenas" in employment and life.

Q: How did your international experience impact your personal or academic growth?

A: It has for sure made me more independent since I had to live alone for the first time in my life, but especially more open to different viewpoints and cultures since the city I live in is much more multicultural than Zagreb/Croatia. For the academic growth, I would say it helped me focus on the important aspects of education in general, but my studies as a whole because of the different approach to education in Denmark.

Q: What’s one piece of advice you’d give to someone considering studying abroad?

A: Just do it, don’t be scared because in the end it’s going to be the best experience in your life and it’s going to be something you will remember forever.


~Antun Zelić

Q: What was your biggest challenge while studying abroad, and how did you overcome it?

A: Honestly, the biggest challenge was dealing with all the requested paperwork regarding the Erasmus+ scholarship, and getting fast and sufficient answers from my home and host institutions. I overcame it by doing the research, asking a lot of questions and being determined in my goal to study abroad.

Q: What’s your favorite memory from your time abroad?

A: It's really hard to choose one, but I think it would be the last dinner and night out I had with all of my flatmates. Even though it was really sad because our first flatmate was leaving the next day, my heart was so filled with love for those people that I felt so happy and grateful to have met them and have the privilege to share my whole Erasmus experience and my home with them.


~Lea Komočar

Q: What's one piece of advice you'd give to someone considering studying abroad?

A: I would advise everyone to study abroad, as I believe it’s an excellent opportunity to not only explore a new environment but also to get to know themselves better. It allows you to embrace everything that’s offered to you, so you can later implement those experiences and knowledge in your own country.

Q: What's your favorite memory from your time abroad?

A: My favorite memory from my time abroad is a sampling trip we had in Northern Spain. During the trip, my friend and I accidentally bumped our assistant, and he fell off the boat into a reservoir. In the end, we all ended up swimming together, and it turned into a hilarious and unforgettable experience!


~Lea Raos

Q: What was your biggest challenge while studying abroad, and how did you overcome it?

A: One of the biggest challenges I faced was balancing my social life with academics. Studying in a new country is an amazing experience, but adapting to a different academic system can be tricky. With so many events for Erasmus students, finding that balance became really important.

Q: What's your favorite memory from your time abroad?

A: My Erasmus experience broadened my horizons, taught me to solve problems independently, gave me the chance to study in English and improve it, opened up professional opportunities, and introduced me to amazing people who taught me so much and became my friends.


~Lucija Marić
